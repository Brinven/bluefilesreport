The G-Engines Are Coming article appeared in the November 1956 issue of Young Men magazine.

The article by Mike Gladych "The G-Engines are Coming." "The article begins with a claim that airplanes will be made obsolete. Many aircraft companies were engaged in the study of the control of gravitation including Glenn L. Martin Aircraft Co., Convair, Bell Aircraft, Lear Inc., and Sperry Gyroscope. The article had quotes from aircraft industry executives Lawrence D. Bell, "We're already working with nuclear fuels and equipment to cancel out gravity.", and George Trimble, stating that he thought the effort to conquer gravity, "could be done in about the time it took to build the first atom bomb." William P. Lear, "All matter within the ship would be influenced by the ship's gravitation only. This way, no matter how fast you accelerated or changed course, your body would not feel it any more than it now feels the tremendous speed and acceleration of the earth."

According to Gladych, all this would be done through recent experimental advances in particle physics, "In the course of such experiments, Dr. Stanley Deser and Dr. Richard Arnowitt of the Princeton Institute of Advanced Study found the gravity culprit—tiny particles responsible for gravitation. Without those G-(gravity) particles, an atom of say, iron still behaved like any other iron atom except for one thing—it was weightless."

In 1957 a conference on the role of gravitation in physics was held at Chapel Hill at the University of North Carolina. The conference was funded by Wright Air Development Center, a U.S. Air Force funding body. (The report's official designation is: WADC Technical Report 57-216) Cecile DeWitt then edited the recorded material into its final form. The U.S. Air Force wanted to bring together the top thinkers in physics and discuss theoretically could gravity be harnessed.

See The Role of Gravitation in Physics: Report from the 1957 Chapel Hill conference. Edition open access 2017.

From the Abstract: "From January 18-23, 1957, A group of physicists from several countries met at the University of North Carolina to discuss the role of gravitation in physics. The program was divided into two broad headings: Unquantized and quantized General Relativity. Under the former came a review of classical relativity, its experimental test, the initial value problem, gravitational radiation, equations of motion, and unified field theory. Under quantized General Relativity came a discussion of the motivation for quantization, the problem of measurement, and the actual techniques for quantization. In Both sections the relationship of General Relativity to fundamental particles was discussed. In addition, there was a session devoted to cosmological questions."

Reported by Michael Schratt.

THEN IT ALL WENT BLACK.

In 1960's newspaper articles on gravity control stopped, nothing else was done about the subject of harnessing gravity publicly. In fact, the topic was played down and ridiculed in academia.

Picture is of Don Phillips, Lockheed Skunkworks Engineer retired speaking at the Discloser Project at the National Press Club in Washington D.C. on the record in May 2001. His full interview is documented in Steven M. Greer Disclosure book. I want to site a few of his most telling quotes.

"When I was working with the Skunkworks, we signed an agreement with the National Security Agency, and the National Security Council, as well as the C.I.A. And we kept very quiet about things."

Dr. Greer asked if anti-gravitational research was going on. Mr. Phillips answered "The propulsion system that you are asking about. I heard that it existed."

"We know that there were some captured craft from 1947 in Roswell, New Mexico. And, yeah, they are real. And we really did get some technology from them."
