The crew was made up of Make Rogers (the groups work Four-men), Travis Walton, Ken Peterson, Allen Dalis, Dwayne Smith, John Goulette, and Steve Pierce. After a hard day's work, they loaded up the old dirty brown 1965 International double-cab truck, and at about 6:10 pm they headed back to Snowflake. While they were driving along the rough dirt road from the work site, off to their right they saw a yellowish glow through the trees. Guesses what it was, was the topic, they thought maybe light from a hunters' camp, headlights or maybe a fire. As they drove up the hill there was an opening in the trees, Mike stopped the truck, Travis throw open his door so they could see, Dalis yelled "It's a flying saucer!"

There hovering silently, motionless, about fifteen feet above a tangled pile of logs was a strange golden disc, casting an eerie glow illuminating the immediate area. As they sat watching spellbound time seemed to freeze. Travis glanced at the others who were starting in awe. When he suddenly had the urge to see the craft up close. He quickly got out of the truck and started toward the hovering craft. This alarmed the others and they called for him to come back. As Travis got closer to the craft the calls for him to return got louder. But Travis was not persuaded, he crouched down behind a log almost under the craft. As he was looking up, he could hear a faint sound it was high pitched overlaying a low ton. The sound then got louder, and the craft began to wobble like a top. Travis felt it was about to leave so he started to stand up and head back to the truck. When suddenly a tremendously bright, blue-green beam of light shot from the bottom of the craft striking Travis in the head and chest, knocking him unconscious, and throwing him back ten feet through the air. The others thought that light killed Travis, panicked and drove away. After driving for a few minutes, they stopped and came to their senses, they were going to go for help. As they drove on, Mike decided to go back to help Travis, he told the others they could stay there if they wanted, but they all went along. They searched the area where they saw Travis fall but did not find anything. They headed to Heber, a small town, arguing along the way about how to report what happened, fearing no one would believe them. They arrived in the quiet town and found a pay phone. Ken Peterson placed the call to the sheriff at 7:35 pm. Deputy Ellison met with them and after hearing their story contacted Sheriff Marlin Gillespie. After hearing

the story and having Travis's home checked for him. They conducted a search of the area where Travis had gone missing, finding nothing. Several extensive searches were done throughout the coming days. The men were suspected of murder, they took and passed a lie detector test, but still people did not believe them.

Meanwhile Travis had been taken about the craft. He was examined or given medical treatment or both. He started to come to and found himself lying on a table with a bright light over him. He was in excruciating pain from head to toe. He found it strange that he still had on his clothes, jacket, and boots. The room was warm and humid there was a device covering his chest and emitting a soft green glow. The room was strangely shaped and in a dark gray metal color. At first, he thought he was in a hospital, he could see three figures in the room he thought were doctors. As he focused on them to his horror, they were not human. They had white marshmallow-looking skin, small bodies maybe five foot in height, large bald heads, small jaws, narrow thin mouths, small, rounded noses, tiny, crinkled ear lobes, and large lash-less eyes with brown irises twice the size of a human. This description matched the E.T.'s known as Reticulin's. He grabbed a clear rod off a shelf and held it as if he was going to hit them. They stood back by the door, their arms and hands outstretched, it was a standoff. After a few moments, as Travis was about to try to rush by them, they backed away and left down the hall, leaving him alone in the room. After a few moments Travis stepped to the open door and looked out into a curved hallway. He decided to go the other way from the Aliens. He found an open door to his right that opened into a large room with an odd, shaped chair in the middle of it. As he approached the chair, he was relieved to see it was empty. During his approach the room darkened, and a star field appeared around him as the floor, walls, and ceiling seemed to go away. He looked at the chair and there was one small green screen and a block of buttons. He pushed one and nothing seemed to happen. A few minutes later he felt that someone else was in the room. He turned and saw a human man standing in the doorway dressed in a blue jump-suit with a clear glass orb on his head. He felt a sense of calm and relief that maybe someone from the military came to rescue him. The man told him to be calm and come with him. Travis did not remember his mouth moving, but he went with him. They walked out of the craft into what could have been a curved hanger. There were two other saucers that he could see in there. They walked through an open door down a hallway and into a room with another man and women, also dressed in blue jump-suits near a table in the center.

They put him up on the table and the women put a clear mask over his mouth and he passed out. When he awoke his pain was gone and he was laying on the side of a road with a dark saucer over him. A light on the bottom turned off and it flew straight up at a high rate of speed making no sound. He got up and walked to a phone booth and called Mike Rogers. His ordeal was over, though he would never forget it.

On December 29, 1980, Betty Cash, Vickie Landrum, and Vickie's seven-year-old grandson, Colby, were returning home after dinner on State Road 1485, near Dayton, Texas, on the outskirts of Houston. At around 9 PM, according to Vickie Landrum, they noticed a bright light in the sky. The light continued to get closer, until it positioned itself directly in front of their vehicle. Betty slammed on the brakes, and their car came to an abrupt halt. The entire area was lit up like it was daytime, and they could see the outline of a diamond shaped craft.

The object measured approximately 90 feet tall and resembled two ice-cream cones attached at the widest end. There appeared to be a flat section in the center which included multiple illuminated windows. It was cold outside, but the intense heat from the object permeated the interior of their car. Betty eventually got out of the vehicle and walked to the front of her car to get a better look at the object. It was bobbing up and down, and she could hear an intense "beeping" noise emanating from the object. She could see a blue colored flame emanating from its lower section. Each time the flame exited the bottom section, she could hear a loud "roar." After a few minutes looking at the craft, Betty walked back to the driver's side door and when she reached for the handle, she was severely burned. Using the fabric of her coat, she was able to get back inside the vehicle. The temperature inside the car had reached an unbearable point, and Betty had to turn on the air conditioner.

After a few minutes, the mysterious object departed the area, and all three witnesses observed 23 double-rotor Chinook helicopters. These had spotlights on as though they were looking for something. In addition, there appeared to be at least one UH1 Huey helicopter, and one large Sikorsky "Sky-crane".

Immediately after the event, all three witnesses suffered from the effects of radiation poisoning. These symptoms included headaches, diarrhea, loss of appetite, lumps on the neck, upset stomach, dehydration, and swollen lips. These adverse effects may have been due to a hull breach inside the reactor of the craft, thereby contaminating the area with dangerous radiation. Attempts by the primary witnesses to have their case investigated in a court of law were thrown out due to "lack of evidence," yes, the government denied the incident in its entirety.
