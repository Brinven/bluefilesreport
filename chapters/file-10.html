Roswell and Project Mogul

The official U.S. Air Force explanation, detailed in two 1990s reports ("The Roswell Report: Fact vs. Fiction in the New Mexico Desert," 1995, and "Case Closed," 1997), attributes the 1947 incident to debris from a top-secret Project Mogul balloon train (Flight No. 4, launched June 4, 1947). Project Mogul aimed to detect Soviet nuclear tests via high-altitude acoustic sensors; its classified nature led to the initial "weather balloon" cover story.

Within the UFO community and some independent analyses (e.g., Reddit discussions, articles in Daily Mail 2024 citing researchers like Dr. David Rudiak), the Mogul explanation is often rejected or debated. Critics argue discrepancies in flight logs (e.g., claims Flight No. 4 was canceled) or that balloon debris does not fully match witness descriptions of unusual materials. These critiques appear in non-peer-reviewed books, websites (e.g., roswellproof.com), and forums, not established scientific journals.

Skeptics and proponents both reference Mogul, but acceptance is divided: official and historical sources endorse it as the resolution, while UFO researchers view it as insufficient or part of a cover-up. No peer-reviewed scientific consensus debunks or confirms extraterrestrial involvement.
