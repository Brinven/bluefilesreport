UFOs have unusual and distinctive flight characteristics. Let us look at these types of sighting through a technical lens.

Hovering

A favorite posture of UFOs is to hang nearly motionless in the sky or at very low altitude. Obviously, objects do not depend upon aerodynamic lift as do conventional, fixed-wing aircraft. Neither are they suspended from large, rotating blades like helicopters. The absence of blasts of air or billows of smoke rules out jet engines and rockets.

These vehicles are not defeating gravity entirely. If it did, it would not hover; instead, it would float upward in the atmosphere. The vehicle is using its propulsion system to stay buoyant. The UFO balances in the air vertically between a buoyant force upward, equal to the weight of air it displaces, and an equal force downward, provided by gravity. The magnitude of the buoyant force, amounting to the volume of the UFO times the density of displaced air. The hovering UFO, therefore, almost defeats gravity but not completely. The reduction in weight and mass is about 99.3%.

Descent and Ascent

Landing, the common manner of UFO descent for almost every observed landing, the UFO maneuver 50 ft. above the ground. Then executes a maneuver what is commonly called "falling leaf" or "pendulum." This seems to be the preferred method of control.

The pilot may achieve this by simply tilting the craft slightly to one side by a small amount. The force holding the craft in the air would then point in a direction making an angle with the vertical, but the effective weight would continue to act straight down. The craft would sink slowly as it moved sideways in the direction of tilt. Then the pilot would reverse this action to the other side of the craft until close to the ground then let the controls come back to neutral position.

Take off, almost every observed departure goes through this two-stage maneuver, namely, a cautious rise of 50 to 100 ft. followed by a spectacular blast-off.

The vehicle does stop for a few moments at the desired height and seems to undulate before taking off.

Acceleration and Relativity

One of the most prominent features of UFOs is their ability to change speed and direction in flight much more quickly than is possible for an airplane. This manner of flight may not only be a characteristic of UFOs but essential to their mode of flight.

The fundamental problem in understanding UFOs is that their peculiar flight pattern involves enormous accelerations for which adequate forces are not in evidence. The resolution of this dilemma appears to lie in the physical property of inertia.

The Theory of Relativity has become indispensable to every branch of modern physical sciences. In this theory Einstein recognized the inseparable relationship between gravitation and inertia. Within this theory Einstein determined it is not possible to distinguish inertial forces from gravitational forces.

To put this point in another perspective, inertial mass and gravitational mass cannot be distinguished; the property of matter that reacts to accelerations is the same as that which responds to gravitational fields.

Now to the bearing of these matters upon UFOs. If, as has been suggested, UFOs nullify the effect of gravity upon their mass, it must follow that they will nullify their inertial behavior also.

The flight patterns of UFOs are thoroughly consistent with, and even demand, a great reduction of their inertial, mass. And these characteristics occur in conjunction with a corresponding reduction of their response to gravity. One must conclude that the full range of UFO performance is internally consistent and in complete accord with General Relativity. This finding is even more remarkable since few, if any, of the witnesses were theoretical physicists.

Undulation

In horizontal flight, UFOs display an odd habit that nags the rational mind, namely, bobbing up and down in a sinusoidal path. What is clear is that the weight of a UFO at rest on the ground is greatly reduced while in flight.

Consider a UFO in horizontal flight with its effective mass being very small, depending upon the altitude. All the vertical forces are in balance, with gravity acting downward upon the effective mass. If the UFO should fly over a local area in which the strength of gravity was somewhat greater, it would temporarily experience an increase in the downward force. It would respond by a displacement downward, a slight loss of altitude. So, if this is true then it would move slightly upward in passing over a zone of relatively weak gravity. The gravitational strength near the surface of the earth is nearly constant. It is slightly less at sea than on the continents, and the earth's rotation causes a small change that is dependent upon latitude. This bobbing up and down is what has been observed by many witnesses.

Categorized as follows:

- 1. Sudden and instantaneous acceleration: Objects moving in such a manner that they are capable of maneuvering suddenly, deliberately, and sometimes in the opposite direction. Specific signatures normally include acoustic, heat, and electromagnetic and are traditionally recognized as a sonic boom, vapor contrails, and atmospheric ionization. Currently, even the world's most advanced military and reconnaissance aircraft have detectible signatures.

In many cases it is nearly impossible to actually see the object and instead reports often include what is seen around the object.

- 4. Trans-medium travel: Objects that have the ability to travel easily in various environments and conditions seemingly without any change in performance capabilities. Our current understanding of physics requires vehicles to be designed specifically according to their application. For the reason, there are stark differences between those vehicles that orbit in space, fly in the atmosphere, and travel in the sea.
